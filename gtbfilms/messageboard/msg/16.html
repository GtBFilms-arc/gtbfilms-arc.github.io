It is an interesting suggestion, what could possible go wrong, drinking lager in the shower? Although, I am not sure that it is worth making too many more 1 minute films, unless they are for a specific competition, or like you say, contained within a larger film. This is because it took us a good few hours to do 'Identity Theft', and we got a very short film out of it, compare with our longer films, which took longer to film, but not THAT much longer.

From the other competition entries, 'Don't drive backwards down the motorway', "Stop, Look, Zombie!" and "Never look at an electricity pylon through binoculars" were the best, doing something like that could be fun, but needs to be suitably bizarre - never eat pickled onions while attempting to drive round a roundabout, or something like that.

My only concern is the useability of what we produce, plus the fact there are no deadlines, which GtBFilms appears to need. Maybe we would be better looking for competitions and that and attempting to produce something specifically for them.

There lists of various film festivals and that on the IAC website:

http://www.fvi.org.uk/index.html

Or here's a thing to create a wee advert video for the excellent free web browser firefox:

http://www.spreadfirefox.com/

Hmmm, we have only got 15 days left to make a film for that one!

There are some links to film making competitions at:

http://www.screensouth.org/pages/news/news_headlines.html

To stick to a Scotland competition, we could do something (I don't think there is any restriction on title/subject/type of film) for next yea**cottish Association of Moviemakers competition - from this web site, I see that at least a couple of films there are by members of ECVS, and almost all the films are documentaries:

http://www.fvi.org.uk/regions/Sam_2005.html

I think making stuff specifically for competitions is more likely to get us moving!
