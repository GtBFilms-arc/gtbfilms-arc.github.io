Management Speak

Submitted by Baldas on 20/Apr/2007
127.0.0.1

Message:

This isn't a plot idea, this is just a wee eccentricity that a character in a film could have. Something happened at my work yesterday that I found amusing and I thought we could use. The boss likes his buzzwords and management speak (as do all bosses) but he got a bit mixed up at one point and said "Let's make sure we're all p*ssing up the same flag pole". After that there was an amused pause, and he said, "Erm, that's a different version, but you know what I mean". Well, OUR character could do this sort of thing, but without realising his mistakes, in a Del Boy kind of fashion.

Examples (in addition to the above one, which I think is great):

1. "We need to think outside the box" AND "Don't reinvent the wheel" --> becomes --> "We need to think outside the wheel" OR "Don't re-invent the box".

2. "You can't make an omelette without breaking eggs" AND "Do some blue-sky thinking" --> becomes --> "You can't make an omelette without some blue sky thinking".

There must be loads of possibilities. A quick Google for "management speak" helped me create these. The best bit of all is that it makes fun of management. It could be Norris in What A Mess 2 that says these things, i.e. he is a bit of a sponjol so he is ideal.
