Mage's Spellbook
Spell Blurb List

ACID BLAST

As this spell is cast a deluge of corrosive fluid smites the chosen target causing unpleasant burns. Although the spell causes less damage to creatures than some of the other magical attacks, it also causes extensive damage to weapons, armour and other equipment.

The target for this spell is the monster type, monster group, or player group to be affected.


AWAKEN

An effective spell against most kinds of magical or drug induced slumber. If cast successfully any characters affected will immediately wake up.

Spell target is the monster type, monster group, character, or player group to be affected.


BALL OF FIRE

With this spell a mage can summon a searing ball of flames to scorch his enemies. Although summoned by magic the flames are ordinary in other respects and will therefore not affect creatures immune to ordinary fire. The flames will cause damage to any wooden or flammable material carried by the target.

Target for this spell is the monster type, monster group, or player group to be affected.


BATTLE LUST

A powerful but risky spell. When cast the mage's own group will fight with berserk disregard for danger, injury or pain. Their combat strength will be higher than usual and their obvious insanity may well cause timid enemy groups to flee in terror. The spell causes your group to ignore your own retreat factors and there is a good chance of serious losses if the battle goes against you.

The target for this spell is '99'


BLACK VOID

A deadly offensive spell against creatures of all kinds. When the spell is cast a magical gate is opened into another plane. Anyone, or thing, close to the gate is sucked towards it and will be lost for ever in the void. The pull is fortunately not too powerful and most creatures will be strong enough to resist it unless they are unlucky or very close.

The target for this spell is the monster type, monster group or player group to be affected.


CHOKING GAS

With this spell a mage summons a dense cloud of gas to plague his enemies. Weak opponents will find it almost impossible to breath and their combat strength will be reduced. More powerful opponents will be affected less and undead creatures not at all.

The target for this spell is the monster type, monster group, or player group to be affected.


CONFUSION I, II

Although they cause no physical damage these are very effective combat spells against all creature types. The target is confused and is unable to think straight which makes fighting very difficult. CONFUSION I affects just one creature whereas CONFUSION II will affect several.

Spell target is the monster type, monster group, or player group to be affected.


CURE DISEASE

A useful spell for the curing of most illnesses but not all. The most potent diseases will be eased but not wholly cured. Only a devout priest of Baldor can cure these. The spell will not restore toughness lost as a result of the illness.

Spell target is the character number of the ill character.


DEFLECT MAGIC I, II, III

The three DEFLECT MAGIC spells provide useful protection against attacks by enemy mages. They reduce the effectiveness of any magical spells cast against the users group. DEFLECT MAGIC I is the weakest of these spells and DEFLECT MAGIC III the most powerful. There is a small chance that the magic deflected will actually be reflected towards the mage who cast it.

The spell target is '99'.


DESTROY UNDEAD I, II

The DESTROY UNDEAD spells are the most cost effective method for mages wishing to remove undead creatures from a combat. Only the weakest undead creatures will be destroyed outright but others will suffer considerable damage. The spell affect all undead creatures in the targeted group.

The target for the spell is the type or group number of the monster to be affected


DETECT TREASURE I,II,III

Information spells which provide the location of any nearby buried treasure. The spells only work in the wilderness. DETECT TREASURE I has a range of just a few sectors. The higher level the spells have progressively greater ranges.

No target is required for these spells.


DISINTEGRATE

A powerful offensive spell. The main effect is the destruction of non-living material, i.e. swords, armour and other equipment. As such this spell isn't very effective against creatures relying on claws, teeth or other natural weaponry.

Target for the spell is the monster type, monster group or player group to be affected


DISPEL I, II

Useful spells for dispelling creatures or items. The target will disappear from the mage's location and reappear some distance away. In the wilderness this will usually be several miles off. In a dungeon the target will reappear somewhere else on the same level. The spell does not work in towns.

DISPEL I works on a single creature or item, whereas DISPEL II will affect any number of creatures of the specified type or group.

The target for these spells is the monster type, monster group, player group, item, or item type to be affected. (If DISPEL I is used against a monster group then the target will be chosen at random from within this group)


DRAIN LIFE

A spell to strike fear into the bravest of foes. When cast the spell sucks the vitality (toughness) from the target creature. Weaker creatures will be killed outright but even the strongest of foes will be greatly weakened. Undead creatures are obviously unaffected by this spell.

Spell target is monster type, monster group or player group to be affected.


ENCHANT WEAPONS I, II, III

The ENCHANT WEAPONS spells are eagerly sought after by all warlike groups. Using these spells a mage can temporarily enhance the performance of any weapon type. ENCHANT WEAPONS I provides just a minor improvement. ENCHANT WEAPONS II is more potent but still affects just one character. ENCHANT WEAPONS III also provides a strong enchantment and affects the weapons of your whole group.

The target for spells I and II is the character carrying the weapon to be affected. For spell III the target is '99'.


ENGULFING GLOBE

A black sphere of nothingness appears at the mages command. It then moves randomly, sucking up anything that comes in the way. Once sucked into the globe nothing is ever seen again. The globe is unpredictable and often floats away from the target after sucking up just a few enemies.

Target for the spell is the monster type, monster group, or player group to be affected.


EXIT DUNGEON

Escape is often high on the list of a group's priorities. With this spell a group can teleport from any dungeon location to the wilderness just outside the dungeon entrance.

No target is required.


FEAR I,II

The fear spells cause no physical damage but instead fill the target with terror. FEAR I only affects weak willed opponents causing them to flee. FEAR II affects most opponents, and the weak willed opponents may even be struck dead on the spot out of pure terror!

The target for these spells is the monster type, monster group, or player group to be affected.


FLESH ROT

A spell which most mages save for use against their worst enemies. The spell causes a dreadful rotting disease to afflict one character, or thing, in the target group. Wounds suffered by an afflicted character will fester and rot making healing, even by magical means, impossible until the disease is cured.

The target for this spell is the monster type, monster group or player group to be affected.


HEALING I,II,III,IV

The healing spells are very useful for restoring the health of injured party members. HEALING I and II affect just a single character, but III and IV affect all members of the target group. HEALING II and IV heal more damage than I and III.

The target for I and II is the character to be affected. For III and IV write '99' for your own group.


IDENTIFY ITEM

The ideal spell for finding out what an item is, or what it does. It will reveal all magical properties of an item (and its history, purpose and true name if appropriate).

Spell target is the item to be identified.


INVISIBILITY

One of the most potent defensive spells. Although it only lasts for a short while the mage's group will be totally invisible for the spell's duration. Most enemy creatures or groups will not even realize they exist while the spell is working.

The target is "99".


JAUNT I,II

The Jaunt spells are a specialized form of teleportation devised for use in dungeons. (The presence of so much solid rock makes ordinary teleportation suicidal!) Jaunt I enables a mage to move his group up or down a single dungeon level. Jaunt II enables a mage to move his group to any level within the current dungeon.

Spell target is the level you wish to move to. To Jaunt out of a dungeon write target '0'.


LIGHTNING BLAST

A bolt of electrical energy will strike wherever the mage directs causing burns and shock to any creatures unfortunate to be stood in the way. It is especially effective against opponents clad in metal armour. A few rare creatures will be immune to the effects of this spell.

The target for this spell is the monster type, monster group or player group to be affected.


LOCATE

Avery powerful information spell which reveals information about the location of a particular item, monster or player group. The information may be a direction, distance, name of a town or some other clue.

The spell target it the monster group, player group or item number to be located.


MAGIC BLAST

This is a general purpose magical attack, useful against all kinds of foe. A bust of magical energy will strike without fail causing serious damage.

The target for this spell is the monster type, monster group or player group to be affected.


MAGIC DART

A small dart of magical energy shoots from the mage's hand towards the target. The damage inflicted is small but all creature types are affected and the dart will always strike true.

The target for the spell is the monster type, monster group, or player group to be affected.


MAKE FOOD

Essential for any party lost in the wilderness without food. This spell will provide sufficient food to prevent starvation.

No target is required.


MAP DUNGEON LEVEL

A useful information spell to save time exploring dungeons. The spell provides a complete map of the dungeon level on which it is cast.

No target required


MAP WILDERNESS I,II

These spells can only be used by a group travelling through the wilderness. They will provide mapping information around the sector in which your turn ends.

No target is required for these spells.

PETRIFY

A powerful spell but effective against just one person or creature who will be turned instantly to stone. Any possessions of the petrified person will also be turned to stone. The unfortunate individual will be chosen at random from the target group.

Target for the spell is the monster type, monster group or player group to be affected.


PLAGUE WIND

As this spell is cast a foul pestilence gusts towards the mage's foe. Any creature inhaling the foul air will be infected by a terminal disease which saps toughness and health. Death usually results within several weeks unless a cure can be found. The disease is contagious!

Target for this spell is the monster type, monster group, or player group to be affected.


POISON CLOUD

A poisonous gas cloud will engulf the target, causing damage and reducing the combat strength of all creatures affected. Small or very weak creatures may be killed outright by this spell but undead creatures will suffer no effect at all.

The target for this spell is the monster type, monster group or player group to be affected.


REVEAL MONSTERS

Usable only in dungeons, this spell will reveal the locations of all monsters on the current dungeon level, irrespective of whether you have yet to visit the room.

No target is required.


REVEAL TRAPS

An essential spell for any group hurrying through a dungeon. Cast this spell at the start of your turn and you will be able to detect and avoid most traps along your route. (The location of the traps will be reported for future reference.) The spell will also show up trapped items in rooms you pass though during the turn. The spell also reveals secret doors.

No target is required.


REVEAL TREASURE

Very useful when your group is fed up with straining their eyes searching for clues in a dark dingy dungeon. This spell will reveal the location of most of the rooms which contain treasure on the level where your group is located. (This spell only works in dungeons.)

No target is required.


SHIELD I, II, III

These spells are guaranteed to make a mage popular with the fighters in his party. When the spell is cast the recipient's armour is magically enhanced, thus reducing the damage taken in combat. SHIELD I has a fairly minor effect, with SHIELD II and SHIELD III being much stronger. These spells only affect characters with armour.

Spell target is the character to be affected.


SLEEP I,II

Useful spells against the weaker monster types and against other parties. SLEEP I will only affect the weakest monster types, sending them into a deep slumber. SLEEP II works in the same way but will affect more powerful creatures.

Spell target should be the monster type, monster group, or player group to be affected.


SLOW POISON

This will not cure a poisoned character but it will prevent any poison damage for the duration of the turn. The poison remains and will be fully effected on the following turn unless the spell is cast again.

Target for this spell is character number of the poisoned character.


STOP POISON

My poison present in the target character will be completely neutralized. Health lost due to poison damage can only be restored by time or by other healing spells. It will not be restored by the Stop Poison spell it self.

The target for the spell is the character number of the poisoned character.


TARGET ILLUSION

A shimmering haze surrounds the mage's group making it hard for enemy attacks to strike them in missile or melee combat. This spell has no effect against area attacks such as fire balls or lightning bolts.

The spell target is '99'


TELEPORT I,II,III

The teleport spells have two main uses. Getting to somewhere fast and getting out of trouble. The distance covered is a little unpredictable but direction is at the mages control. TELEPORT I will move a group between 1 and 5 sectors, TELEPORT II about 6 to 10, and TELEPORT III about 11 to 15. (Mapping information gained prior to teleportation will be lost. You will only receive a map of movement made after the teleport.)

This target for this spell is the direction to be traveled.


TOTAL DEATH

A spell guaranteed to strike fear in any living creature. When cast in combat one member of the target group, chosen at random, will be killed instantly. Only powerful magical protection can prevent this. This spell dose not affect non-living creatures such as golems or undead.

Spell target is the monster type, monster group, or player group to be affected.


TURN UNDEAD I,II

These necromantic spells allow a mage to protect his party from attacks by undead creatures. When the spell is cast affected creatures will be unable to approach and may even be forced to flee. TURN UNDEAD I only affects the weakest undead types. TURN UNDEAD II is more potent but only a priest using the KILL UNDEAD power can affect the strongest undead creatures.

The target for these spells must be the type number or group number of the monsters you wish to affect.
