Teeth, 1984 - 1986

 Never completed, 'Teeth' was intended to be an educational title for a programming competition. Two sections exist - the educational question and answer section, and the arcade-game maze section. The maze section later made an appearance as a sub-level game in Curse of the Sandwitch. Development of this early title was a great lesson in how collaberative coding can turn into a argumentative shambles with no working end product. The zip archive contains the maze section and the question section as two seperate snapshots as there were developed seperately, with the intention they would be MERGED together once complete.
teeth.zip
(SNA & Z80 files)

DeathDay 2, 1986

 In the spirit of the Biorhythm style type-ins of the time, DeathDay 2 was a program that asked the user a series of questions, then based on their answers and date of birth would predict when they would die. Much hilarity was guaranteed, especially when playing with elderly relatives. This title has a piracy-protection code sheet included.
dd2.zip
(SNA & Z80 files)

Curse of the Sandwitch, 1986



Probably the most well-known of all Brown Software games, 'Curse of the Sandwich' was certainly one of the most popular. It is also worthy of note that this game was the first ever MULTILOAD game from Brown Software! Originally a multi-load, this is the 128K single-load version.
